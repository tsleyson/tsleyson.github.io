Of Night

See the official blog, or buy the first book on Amazon.

James Moran was a college student, floating along through a fourth-rate state college in a small town in Northern California, and spending his spare time writing a local gossip blog while trying to become a real reporter. One day, an unprecedented event happened—a sort of not completely ugly girl named Sophia de Nuit spoke to him after class. Naturally, James suspects she's up to no good, so when he sees her in the seedy part of town, dressed to the nines and going off with an old trucker, he follows her, and discovers that she's actually an immortal succubus that sustains her life and powers by draining the life from men with sex. James and Sophia team up to expose the town pastor, Joseph Austen, who's not only been molesting the children put under his care after school, but is actually an incubus—a male demon capable of turning a woman into a succubus.

I have a second book finished, but I totally ran out of steam with this series. The first blow was the fact that my concept closely resembles awful Syfy Channel series Lost Girl. Then I remembered that The Dresden Files exists, and also discovered The Hollows, and my enthusiasm for the series basically vanished. So it's on indefinite (but probably permanent) hiatus.

The second book will come out whenever I feel like making a cover for it. (My artistic skills may be minimal, but I won't release a book unless I at least try to draw a decent cover for it.) When that happens, the first one will become free. The second book is called God, Country, and Barbecues. The second chapter is up on the blog.
